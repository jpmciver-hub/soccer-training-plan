# Light Strength & Conditioning (Thursday)

**Duration:** 30-40 minutes  
**Goal:** Build functional strength, core stability, balance, and injury prevention  
**Equipment:** Resistance band (light), mat or grass surface

> This is bodyweight-only and light resistance work. No heavy weights for a 10-year-old. The focus is on movement quality, core strength, balance, and building the athletic foundation that supports speed, agility, and endurance on the field.

---

## Warm-Up (5 min)

| Exercise | Duration |
|----------|----------|
| Jog in place | 1 min |
| Arm circles (forward + backward) | 30 sec each |
| Hip circles | 30 sec each direction |
| Bodyweight squats (slow) | 10 reps |
| Walking lunges | 10 each leg |

---

## Core Session (25-30 min)

### Block 1: Lower Body (10 min)

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Coaching Points |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Bodyweight squats** | 3 x 12 | Feet shoulder-width, weight in heels, chest up. Full depth -- thighs parallel or below. |
| **Split squats** | 3 x 8 each leg | Rear foot elevated on a step or low bench if available. Front knee tracks over toes. |
| **Lateral lunges** | 2 x 8 each side | Step wide, sit hips back on the lunging leg. Push back to start. Great for groin strength. |
| **Single-leg RDL** | 2 x 8 each leg | Stand on one leg, hinge at hips, opposite leg extends back. Light touch on ground for balance. Develops hamstrings and balance. |
| **Calf raises** | 2 x 15 | Edge of a step if available. Full range -- rise high, lower below step level. |

### Block 2: Core (10 min)

| Exercise | Sets x Duration/Reps | Coaching Points |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Plank** | 3 x 30 sec | Elbows under shoulders, body straight, squeeze glutes. No sagging hips. |
| **Side plank** | 2 x 20 sec each side | Stack feet or stagger. Hips up, straight line from head to feet. |
| **Dead bugs** | 3 x 8 each side | Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90. Extend opposite arm and leg, keep lower back pressed into ground. |
| **Bicycle crunches** | 2 x 12 each side | Slow and controlled. Elbow toward opposite knee. Don't pull on neck. |
| **Superman hold** | 3 x 15 sec | Lie face down, lift arms and legs off ground. Squeeze glutes and lower back. |

### Block 3: Balance & Resistance Band (5-10 min)

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Coaching Points |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Single-leg balance** | 2 x 30 sec each leg | Eyes closed for progression. Challenge: parent gently pushes from different angles. |
| **Band lateral walks** | 2 x 10 each direction | Band around ankles. Stay low in athletic stance, step sideways. Builds hip stability. |
| **Band monster walks** | 2 x 10 steps forward + back | Band around ankles. Wide stance, walk forward and backward. Keeps tension on the band throughout. |
| **Band pull-aparts** | 2 x 12 | Hold band at chest height, arms extended. Pull apart to sides. Shoulder health and posture. |
| **Single-leg hop and stick** | 2 x 5 each leg | Hop forward on one leg, land and hold balance for 3 seconds. Builds landing control. |

| Activity | Duration |
|----------|----------|
| Child's pose | 30 sec |
| Cat-cow stretch | 30 sec |
| Quad stretch (standing) | 30 sec each leg |
| Hamstring stretch (seated) | 30 sec each leg |
| Hip flexor stretch (half-kneeling) | 30 sec each leg |
| Pigeon stretch | 30 sec each side |

---

## Progression by Phase

| Phase | Adjustment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Weeks 1-3** | Learn all movements with perfect form. No rush. Lower reps if form breaks. Plank 20 sec. |
| **Weeks 4-6** | Increase reps by 2-4. Plank to 30-40 sec. Add balance challenges (eyes closed, unstable surface). |
| **Weeks 7-9** | Add tempo (3 sec down, 1 sec up on squats/lunges). Plank to 45 sec. Single-leg work more frequent. |
| **Weeks 10-12** | Circuit format: move between exercises with minimal rest. Builds conditioning alongside strength. |

---

## Important Notes

- **No heavy weights.** Bodyweight and light bands only at age 10.
- **Form first.** If form breaks, reduce reps or take a rest.
- **This is injury prevention as much as performance.** Strong hips, core, and ankles reduce injury risk significantly.
- Consider 1x/week sessions at [SportHQ](https://sporthq.org/) or [Athletic Republic Cary](https://cary.athleticrepublic.com/) for supervised performance training.
