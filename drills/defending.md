# Defending (Integrated into Wednesday & Saturday)

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes (integrated into 1v1 days or standalone)  
**Goal:** Build sound defensive positioning, tackling confidence, and recovery speed  
**Equipment:** Ball, cones

> At U11 pre-academy level, defending fundamentals are about body shape, patience, and decision-making -- not aggression. The goal is to delay, channel, and win the ball cleanly.

---

## Defensive Principles to Teach

1. **Close down quickly, slow down late** -- sprint to the ball, then chop steps to get balanced as you arrive
2. **Side-on stance** -- angle body to force attacker one direction (usually toward the sideline)
3. **Stay on your feet** -- don't dive in. Patience wins.
4. **Watch the ball, not the body** -- fakes with shoulders/hips don't matter if eyes stay on the ball
5. **Win it, don't just block it** -- tackle to possess, not just to clear

---

## Drills

### Drill 1: Closing Down (10 min)

| Setup | Reps | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 20-yard channel, defender starts 15 yards from attacker | 10 each role | Attacker stands on ball. Defender sprints to close down, decelerates into side-on stance at 2-3 yards. Don't tackle -- just get into correct body shape. Attacker checks: are they side-on? On toes? Low? |

**Progression:** Attacker starts dribbling slowly. Defender closes, matches speed, and mirrors without tackling.

### Drill 2: 1v1 Defending (10 min)

| Setup | Reps | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 10x15 yard grid, cone gate behind defender | Continuous, 2-min rounds | Attacker tries to dribble through the gate. Defender's job: stay between attacker and gate, channel to one side, wait for a heavy touch to poke the ball away. |

**Coaching Points:**
- Bend the run to angle the attacker toward the sideline
- Patience -- jockey and wait, don't lunge
- Poke tackle when attacker's touch is heavy or they look down

### Drill 3: Recovery Runs (5 min)

| Setup | Reps | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Attacker starts on halfway, defender starts 5 yards behind and to the side | 6 | On "go," attacker dribbles toward goal. Defender sprints to recover, gets goal-side, then defends 1v1. |

**Key Point:** The recovery run is at an angle -- sprint toward your own goal first to get goal-side, then turn and face the attacker.

### Drill 4: Tackle Technique (5 min)

| Type | Reps Each Foot | Description |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Block tackle** | 5 | Face to face, step in with inside of foot, firm ankle, body weight behind the tackle. Practice on a slowly rolling ball first. |
| **Poke tackle** | 5 | From a jockeying position, extend far foot to poke ball away from attacker. Quick jab, recover stance. |

---

## Progression by Phase

| Phase | Adjustment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Weeks 1-3** | Body shape only. Close down and hold position. No tackling pressure. |
| **Weeks 4-6** | Add jockeying at jogging speed. Introduce poke tackle. |
| **Weeks 7-9** | Full 1v1 defending. Attacker at 75-100% speed. Recovery runs. |
| **Weeks 10-12** | 2v1 scenarios: two attackers, one defender. Delay and channel until support arrives (imaginary or real). |

---

## Integration

Defending drills fit naturally into:
- **Wednesday 1v1 sessions** -- attacker drills become defender drills when roles swap
- **Saturday scrimmage** -- coach specific defensive moments during play
- **Speed & agility days** -- recovery runs double as sprint work
